# Copy and Conversion Rules

## Purpose

This file defines customer-facing copy, CTA, fulfilment, and conversion rules for RBP Shopify storefront work.

Use this guidance when editing page copy, product copy, service copy, onboarding forms, cart guidance, membership content, marketplace pages, Business NBN pages, finance/insurance pages, help center items, or account/member portal messaging.

The goal is to keep the storefront practical, clear, commercially credible, and aligned with RBP's service model. The store should help customers understand what to buy, what happens next, and when review, onboarding, scoping, or fulfilment is required.

## Core voice principles

Customer-facing copy should be:

- Clear.
- Practical.
- Specific.
- Calm.
- Trustworthy.
- Action-oriented.
- Suitable for Australian SMEs.
- Honest about review, setup, delivery, and eligibility.

Avoid copy that is:

- Generic.
- Hype-driven.
- Overly abstract.
- Internally focused.
- Full of unexplained product taxonomy.
- Overpromising.
- Too casual for commercial services.
- Too technical for normal business customers.

## Storefront positioning

RBP is not just a retail catalogue.

The storefront should present RBP as a guided business support platform covering:

- Business services.
- Advisory and consulting pathways.
- Templates and document products.
- Documentation suites.
- Toolkits.
- Business NBN.
- Marketplace and partner offers.
- Membership.
- Help and support pathways.
- Future finance and insurance support.

Copy should help customers choose the right pathway, not just describe internal categories.

## Avoid internal taxonomy exposure

Do not make customers decode internal product structures.

Avoid copy like:

```text
Browse our custom.core service verticals and support pathway taxonomy.
```

Prefer:

```text
Choose the type of business support you need and we will guide you to the right next step.
```

Use internal taxonomy only when it is genuinely customer-facing and understandable.

## Fulfilment wording rules

Many RBP products and services require onboarding, review, preparation, scoping, booking, configuration, or manual delivery.

Do not use instant-delivery wording unless the product is genuinely configured for instant access or download.

Avoid:

```text
Instant download
Start immediately
Access everything after checkout
Delivered automatically
Ready as soon as you pay
```

Use when appropriate:

```text
Onboarding required after purchase
Prepared after your onboarding form is submitted
Reviewed before delivery begins
Booking required before confirmation
Scoped before work starts
Serviceability must be confirmed before activation
Delivery begins after intake is reviewed
```

## Product copy rules

Product pages should answer:

- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What is included?
- What happens after purchase?
- Is onboarding required?
- Is review, scoping, booking, or setup required?
- How does the customer get help?

For document/template/toolkit products, clarify whether the customer receives:

- A prepared document.
- A template pack.
- A guided setup process.
- A toolkit requiring onboarding.
- A review or deployment pathway.

For service products, clarify whether the customer is buying:

- A fixed-scope service.
- A review or diagnostic.
- A managed service.
- A booking-based service.
- An enquiry or scoping pathway.

## Service copy rules

Service copy should avoid sounding like generic consulting mist.

Good service copy should describe:

- The business situation.
- The practical outcome.
- The information RBP needs.
- The delivery pathway.
- The next step.

Avoid vague phrases by themselves:

```text
Transform your business
Unlock potential
Drive growth
Seamless solutions
World-class support
End-to-end excellence
```

Use specific practical phrases:

```text
Review the issue, identify the next decision, and prepare a practical action pathway.
```

```text
Submit your business context so RBP can assess the request and recommend the right service pathway.
```

## Onboarding form copy rules

Onboarding forms should make it clear why information is being requested.

Use helpful headings:

```text
Tell us what you need help with
Share your business context
Help us route your request
Provide the details needed to start review
```

Avoid making forms feel like checkout, approval, or service commencement is automatic.

Use wording such as:

```text
Your submission will be reviewed before next steps are confirmed.
```

```text
Submitting this form helps RBP assess your request and prepare the right response.
```

```text
This form does not confirm service delivery, eligibility, availability, or approval until reviewed.
```

## CTA rules

CTAs should match the actual action.

Use `Buy now` only when the product can be purchased directly and the customer understands what happens next.

Use more specific CTAs where appropriate:

```text
Start onboarding
Submit an enquiry
Request review
Check availability
Apply for membership
Start your application
Book a service
View service details
Compare pathways
Ask for guidance
Submit your business context
```

Avoid CTAs that imply false certainty:

```text
Get approved now
Confirm serviceability
Start service immediately
Claim guaranteed access
```

Unless the backend and business process genuinely support that action.

## Membership copy rules

Membership copy should preserve the approved Premium Membership positioning.

Rules:

- Treat membership as application-first.
- Make approval requirements clear.
- Explain that NBN inclusion is part of the paid membership bundle where applicable.
- State that NBN is subject to serviceability, eligibility, provisioning, and related conditions.
- Do not reintroduce generic Foundation / Growth / Premium tiers unless a task explicitly confirms they are live products.
- Avoid implying that every future benefit is guaranteed or currently available.
- Distinguish current benefits from future eligible platform features.

Preferred wording:

```text
Apply for Premium Membership
```

```text
Membership activation is subject to review and approval.
```

```text
Eligible NBN inclusion is subject to serviceability and approval.
```

## Business NBN copy rules

Business NBN copy must be cautious about serviceability.

Avoid:

```text
Your address is approved
Service is available
Connection guaranteed
```

Unless confirmed by the backend and business process.

Use:

```text
Submit your address for review
Serviceability will be confirmed before activation
Availability is subject to network, address, provider, and provisioning checks
```

If the address checker is only a preliminary intake tool, say so.

## Real estate booking copy rules

Real estate/property field service copy should support booking-first, checkout-second logic.

Use:

```text
Select a service and submit the booking details for review.
```

```text
Appointment availability and final confirmation depend on address, service type, time category, and review.
```

Do not imply a booking is confirmed before backend confirmation and payment flow are complete.

## Marketplace and partner offer copy rules

Marketplace copy should position the area as a broader commercial hub, not only a list of partner links.

Partner offer copy should clearly state:

- What the offer is.
- Who it is for.
- Whether the CTA goes to a partner site.
- Any eligibility or availability caveats.
- Whether RBP is providing the service directly or referring the customer to a partner.

Do not render launch-ready CTAs for offers without public-safe URLs.

Avoid hiding external handoff. Make it clear when a user is leaving the RBP storefront.

Useful wording:

```text
This offer is provided by an external partner. Review the partner's terms before proceeding.
```

```text
RBP may receive a referral or affiliate benefit where applicable.
```

Only use affiliate disclosure text where commercially and legally appropriate. Do not invent legal wording if a task requires exact approved disclosure language.

## Finance and insurance copy rules

Finance and insurance copy should be careful, qualified, and enquiry-led.

Avoid:

```text
Guaranteed approval
Best rate guaranteed
Instant finance approval
You are covered
```

Use:

```text
Submit an enquiry so the request can be reviewed.
```

```text
Availability, eligibility, pricing, and approval depend on provider assessment.
```

```text
RBP can help route your enquiry to the relevant pathway where available.
```

Finance and insurance pages should avoid giving regulated advice unless the business process, licensing, and approved copy support it.

## Help center copy rules

Help center content should be:

- Short where possible.
- Step-based when explaining a process.
- Clear about what the customer can do next.
- Honest about review, approval, and processing time.
- Linked to relevant pages where known.

Recommended structure:

```text
Short answer
Steps
What happens next
Related links
Need help
```

Do not bury the answer under brand copy. Help center users are already trying not to be annoyed.

## Account and member portal copy rules

Account copy should explain:

- What the customer purchased.
- What happens next.
- Where to find support.
- Whether onboarding is required.
- Whether membership is active, applied, approved, suspended, or cancelled where the theme can safely infer that state.

Avoid exposing private operational detail.

If customer state is uncertain, use safe generic guidance:

```text
Review your order details and follow any onboarding or support instructions provided by RBP.
```

## Cart guidance rules

Cart guidance should reduce confusion before checkout.

Use cart guidance to explain:

- Onboarding required after purchase.
- Manual fulfilment where applicable.
- Booking or review required where applicable.
- Digital products that are prepared after intake.
- Service products that begin after scoping or onboarding.

Do not block checkout with excessive warning copy.

## SEO copy rules

SEO titles and descriptions should be specific and customer-facing.

Good SEO title pattern:

```text
Business Health Check | Remote Business Partner
```

Good SEO description pattern:

```text
Submit your business context so RBP can review your needs and recommend practical next steps.
```

Avoid generic meta descriptions:

```text
We provide solutions for all your business needs.
```

## Accessibility copy rules

Labels and error messages should be clear.

Use:

```text
Email address is required.
```

Avoid:

```text
Invalid input.
```

Buttons should describe the action:

```text
Submit onboarding request
Apply for membership
Check address
Request a quote pathway
```

Avoid vague buttons:

```text
Go
Click here
Submit
```

unless context makes them clear.

## Risk wording rules

Use qualifying language when the outcome depends on review, provider approval, eligibility, serviceability, or backend confirmation.

Useful words:

```text
subject to
reviewed before
where eligible
where available
may require
final confirmation
provider assessment
serviceability check
```

Do not overuse caveats so much that copy becomes unreadable. State the key condition once near the relevant CTA or claim.

## Before and after examples

Weak:

```text
Get instant access to your business toolkit now.
```

Better:

```text
Purchase the toolkit and complete the onboarding form so RBP can prepare the right setup pathway.
```

Weak:

```text
Check your address and get connected today.
```

Better:

```text
Submit your address for review. Serviceability and activation are confirmed after assessment.
```

Weak:

```text
Claim this partner deal now.
```

Better:

```text
View the partner offer and review the external terms before proceeding.
```

Weak:

```text
Transform your operations with our solution.
```

Better:

```text
Get structured support to review the issue, define the next steps, and improve the process.
```

## Copy review checklist

Before finalizing customer-facing copy, check:

- [ ] Does it say what the customer gets?
- [ ] Does it say what happens next?
- [ ] Does it avoid false instant-delivery claims?
- [ ] Does it avoid overpromising approval, serviceability, availability, or outcomes?
- [ ] Does the CTA match the real action?
- [ ] Does it avoid exposing internal taxonomy unnecessarily?
- [ ] Does it match related cart, account, help, and product messaging?
- [ ] Does it avoid private or sensitive details?
- [ ] Is it specific enough to be useful?
- [ ] Is it simple enough for a busy business owner?

## When exact approved copy is required

Some areas may require exact approved wording:

- Legal terms.
- Privacy notices.
- Finance or insurance disclosures.
- Membership pricing and agreement terms.
- NBN serviceability, terms, or critical information summaries.
- Affiliate disclosures.
- Partner terms.

If exact approved wording is not available, use placeholder copy and document that final legal/commercial review is required.

## Summary

RBP copy should guide customers through complex business products and services without pretending everything is instant, automatic, or guaranteed. Clear next steps beat vague hype. Accurate fulfilment wording beats magical checkout optimism. Humanity may recover if we keep the buttons honest.
